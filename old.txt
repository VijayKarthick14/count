Illegal occupation of government land become a big problem and increasing rapidly. Authorities follow on ground inspection, that is very time consuming thus the encroachments of government land keep on increasing. The reason on increasing encroachment is not the lack of effort, its lack of correct approach using the futuristic tools. Traditional monitoring is performed on paper, physical visits, that generate a report which usually get delayed and time consuming. this increase the damage and cost. In rural area encroachment increasing rapidly that show that this method is not working efficiently. By the time the authorities make a report there is already a construction or wall standing on the land. That leads to a long legal battle, and the public loses its construction and affect the land supposed to be used for the public welfare project like a park, school, road, drainage line, and other. here the real problem is not the public its the absence of the management system that do not track changes in the government land early. System must be quick enough to report it on a early stage and keep official accountable throughout the process.

This project build a GIS based Government Land monitoring and encroachments detection platform. This resolve the problem of physical monitoring. This system store all the Governments land record in a spatial database using the PostgreSQL with PostGIS support. Boundaries are saved as geospatial polygons in GeoJson format and village, taluk and district boundaries can be digitized using QGIS and loaded directly into database. Each land unit is linked with its coordinate (latitude and longitude). The backend runs on Python using Flask or Django, handling data processing, alert and task windows. the frontend uses React with Leaflet to display a interactive map that is easily navigatable by the official and keep track of the land units.

For satellite images, The platform fetches only the marked land unit in the database using the Sentinel-2 through the cloud services, rather than pulling unnecessary dataset. which keeps the system fast and scalable. encroachment of land is detected by comparing the images of same land unit over time. OpenCV handles pixel-level analysis to spot new constructions, expansions, or land cover changes. OpenCV detect every pixel of the land unit and check for any changes such as construction, land boundary, or land cover changes. A CNN model deep dive to classify the features like building, roads, and agriculture.

When the change in different timeline of unit land crosses the threshold, it get plagged. For each flag encroachment score is recalculated based on how land is affected, how close the land unit is growing and whether the land name, history of encroachment attempt. The moment  at which the threshold is crossed, the system auto-draft a legal notice with land details, detection date, satellite evidence.


The platform also calculate the revenue loss caused by the encroachment that affect or delayed the upcoming government schemes on that land. There also a interface where citizen can raise complaint against the suspicious construction through a photo upload. The system cross check the complaint with satellite images then, the system provide a timeline view that shows how land unit has changed month by month using historical imageries, that helps officials to keep reach of only land unit. Every action take to avoid encroachment of government land in the system from detection to notice to resolution is stored completely and permanent into the database. Then do it by yourself.
